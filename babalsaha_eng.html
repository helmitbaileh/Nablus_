What is Bab Al-Sahah

Bab Al-Sahah Square is one of the most famous and oldest squares in the city of Nablus in the northern West Bank, and Palestine in general, and the site is still considered the pulsating heart of Nablus. Residents and experts say that Bab Al-Sahah has a special significance dating back to the time of Saladin and the liberation of the city from the Crusaders. Its importance increased during the Ottoman era, and it continues to be a destination for political and social activities. The square is surrounded by shops, stores, a mosque, and the Ottoman-era Saraya building, and in its heart stands the Bab Al-Sahah Tower built during the reign of Sultan Abdul Hamid II.
